INCIDENT REVIEW REFERENCE #IRC-002
Type: External Industry Reference Case (for internal training and RCA
pattern-matching purposes only; not an incident at this facility)
Source: Industry safety bulletin, referenced by Safety Committee

SUMMARY:
A coke oven battery facility experienced a fatal gas explosion when
entrapped gases accumulated during a period of overlapping maintenance
activity. Gas pressure sensors had recorded elevated readings prior to
the incident, and permit-to-work records showed active work in the
vicinity. However, no system existed to correlate the sensor readings
with the active permit status in real time; the connection was only
identified during the post-incident investigation.

KEY LESSON FOR THIS FACILITY:
This facility's SP-014 (Confined Space Entry) and SP-021 (Hot Work Permit)
procedures already prohibit issuing overlapping permits without a
documented risk assessment (ref OISD-STD-105, doc15). However, near-miss
NM-118 (cross-ref doc17) shows that in practice, gas readings approaching
the threshold are not always cross-checked against active permits in
real time, particularly when fixed gas monitoring coverage is degraded.
The Safety Committee has flagged this as a priority gap: the facility has
the individual data points (gas readings, permit records) but lacks a
system that actively correlates them and raises an alert before a
regulatory threshold is breached, rather than after.

STATUS: Reference case retained for ongoing Safety Committee review of
permit-gas correlation controls.
