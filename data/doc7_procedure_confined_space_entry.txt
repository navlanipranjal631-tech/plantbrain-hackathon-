SAFETY PROCEDURE: SP-014
Title: Confined Space Entry Procedure
Applicable Areas: All tanks, vessels, pits, coke oven battery chambers,
and enclosed process equipment across the facility.
Revision: 4
Effective Date: 1 January 2025

1. PURPOSE
To ensure safe entry into confined spaces by controlling atmospheric,
mechanical, and procedural hazards before and during entry.

2. PRE-ENTRY REQUIREMENTS
2.1 A valid Confined Space Entry Permit must be issued by the Area Safety
Officer before any entry.
2.2 Atmospheric testing is mandatory before entry: Oxygen level must be
between 19.5% and 23.5%. Flammable gas concentration must be below 10% of
Lower Explosive Limit (LEL). Toxic gas levels (H2S, CO) must be below
permissible exposure limits as per Factory Act guidelines.
2.3 Confined space entry permits must NOT be issued concurrently with hot
work permits for the same or adjacent equipment unless a documented risk
assessment confirms no gas migration pathway exists.
2.4 Continuous gas monitoring is required throughout the duration of entry,
with readings logged at intervals not exceeding 30 minutes.

3. DURING ENTRY
3.1 A trained attendant must remain stationed at the entry point at all
times, maintaining continuous communication with personnel inside.
3.2 If gas readings exceed safe limits at any point, entry must be aborted
immediately and all personnel evacuated.

4. RESPONSIBILITY
Area Safety Officer is responsible for permit issuance. Shift Supervisor is
responsible for verifying no conflicting hot work permits are active in the
vicinity before authorizing entry.
