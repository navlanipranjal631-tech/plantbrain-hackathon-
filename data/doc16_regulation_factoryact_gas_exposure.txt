REGULATORY REFERENCE EXCERPT
Standard: Factory Act - Permissible Exposure Limits (Toxic & Flammable Gas)
Relevant Clause Summary (for internal reference use):

1. Oxygen levels in any confined space must be maintained between 19.5%
and 23.5% for safe entry.
2. Hydrogen Sulphide (H2S) exposure must not exceed 10 ppm (8-hour
time-weighted average); short-term exposure must not exceed 15 ppm for
more than 15 minutes.
3. Carbon Monoxide (CO) exposure must not exceed 50 ppm (8-hour
time-weighted average).
4. Flammable gas concentration must remain below 10% of the Lower
Explosive Limit (LEL) for any occupied or work area.
5. Continuous monitoring is required where historical data indicates
gas accumulation risk, including coke oven battery areas and confined
vessel interiors.

These limits form the basis for atmospheric testing requirements specified
in SP-014 (Confined Space Entry Procedure).
