SAFETY PROCEDURE: SP-002
Title: Emergency Response and Evacuation Procedure
Applicable Areas: Entire facility.
Revision: 6
Effective Date: 1 January 2025

1. PURPOSE
To ensure a coordinated, rapid response to any confirmed gas leak, fire,
explosion, or other emergency condition, minimizing harm to personnel.

2. TRIGGER CONDITIONS
Emergency response is to be initiated immediately upon:
2.1 Any fixed or portable gas detector reading exceeding 10% LEL, or
toxic gas levels exceeding Factory Act permissible limits (see SP-031,
regulatory reference doc16).
2.2 Confirmed fire or explosion, regardless of scale.
2.3 Loss of structural integrity of any pressure vessel or gas line.

3. RESPONSE STEPS
3.1 Sound area alarm and initiate evacuation of the affected zone and
adjacent zones within 50 meters.
3.2 Shift Supervisor to notify Emergency Response Team and Area Safety
Officer immediately.
3.3 All active permits (hot work, confined space entry) within the
affected zone must be suspended immediately.
3.4 Emergency Response Team to assess and contain the incident; external
agencies (fire brigade, DGMS as applicable) to be notified per statutory
timelines.
3.5 A preliminary incident report must be filed within 24 hours,
documenting sensor readings, timeline, and response actions taken.

4. RESPONSIBILITY
Shift Supervisor is responsible for initiating the first response.
Emergency Response Team Lead is responsible for on-scene coordination.
