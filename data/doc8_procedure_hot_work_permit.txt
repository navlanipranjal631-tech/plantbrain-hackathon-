SAFETY PROCEDURE: SP-021
Title: Hot Work Permit Procedure
Applicable Areas: All areas where welding, cutting, grinding, or any
spark/flame-generating activity is performed.
Revision: 3
Effective Date: 1 January 2025

1. PURPOSE
To prevent fire and explosion hazards arising from hot work activities in
proximity to flammable materials or gas-hazard areas.

2. PRE-WORK REQUIREMENTS
2.1 A Hot Work Permit must be issued by the Area Safety Officer, valid for
a single shift only.
2.2 The work area must be checked for flammable gas concentration before
work begins; readings must be below 10% LEL.
2.3 Hot work permits must NOT be issued within 15 meters of any active gas
pressure alarm zone or any location with an unresolved elevated gas reading
in the preceding 4 hours.
2.4 A fire watch with a charged extinguisher must be present for the
duration of hot work and for 30 minutes after work completion.

3. CROSS-REFERENCE
This procedure must be read in conjunction with SP-014 (Confined Space
Entry Procedure) when hot work is planned near confined space entry points,
as simultaneous permits in the same zone are a recognized high-risk
combination and are restricted under this facility's permit-to-work policy.

4. RESPONSIBILITY
Area Safety Officer is responsible for verifying gas readings and checking
for conflicting active permits before issuing a hot work permit.
